Marine Pollution Reduction Project:

To reduce marine pollution using a garbage cleaning robot and turbidity sensor, you can deploy the robot in water bodies to autonomously collect and dispose of floating debris. The turbidity sensor can help identify areas with high pollutant concentrations. By combining these technologies, you create an effective system for targeted pollution cleanup and prevention. Regular maintenance and data analysis are crucial to optimize the robot's efficiency in addressing specific pollution sources.

OCEAN'S IMPACT ON HEALTH

The health of our oceans directly correlates with human well-being. Clean water from the oceans is essential for drinking, sanitation, and agriculture, impacting global health. Seafood, a significant protein source for many, contributes to nutrition and sustenance. Furthermore, the oceans play a crucial role in maintaining air quality, influencing climate patterns that affect respiratory health. Preserving ocean health is not just an environmental concern; it is a fundamental aspect of safeguarding human health and ensuring a sustainable future.
